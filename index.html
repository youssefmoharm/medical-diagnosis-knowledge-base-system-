CONFLICT RESOLUTION STRATEGY

1

URGENCY RANK — Primary Sort (Descending)

Critical (3) > High (2) > Medium (1) > Low (0). The most life-threatening condition always wins regardless of confidence. Demonstrated in T06: 8 rules fired, Critical Heart Attack beats all others.

2

CONFIDENCE SCORE — Tiebreaker (Descending)

When urgency ties (e.g. T03: COVID-19 and Asthma both High), confidence selects the winner. R02 at 90% beats R06 at 88%. All tied losers become "alternatives" for clinician review.

3

TRANSPARENCY — Full WHY + HOW Explanation

Every result includes: (a) WHY — which facts triggered which rules with all conditions listed; (b) HOW — step-by-step trace of pattern matching → conflict resolution → primary selection → alternatives. No unexplained outputs.

WHY EXPLANATION

  • Lists all active facts detected from inputs
  • For each fired rule: required conditions, urgency level, confidence %
  • Explicitly states which conditions matched and why
  • When no rules fire: clearly states no match and why

HOW EXPLANATION

  • Step 1: Pattern matching — how many facts evaluated, how many rules matched
  • Step 2: Conflict resolution — urgency groupings shown
  • Step 3: Primary selection — winner with full justification
  • Step 4: Alternative diagnoses — all other fired rules ranked
🔲

BINARY SYMPTOM ENCODING — NO SEVERITY GRADATION

All symptoms are boolean (present/absent). A "mild cough" and a "severe cough" are encoded identically. This prevents the system from discriminating symptom severity, which is clinically significant.

📏

SPARSE RULE COVERAGE — DIAGNOSTIC GAPS

Only 10 rules cover a narrow slice of conditions. Common presentations like sinusitis, UTI, diabetic episodes, allergic reactions, or panic attacks produce no_match — leaving patients without guidance.

⚠️

T11 — CLINICALLY AMBIGUOUS NO-MATCH

Cough + Sore Throat + Fever produces no_match because R05 (Cold) excludes fever. But this presentation could be flu (R01 needs fatigue) or COVID (R02 needs shortness of breath). The patient receives no guidance — a potential safety gap.

🎯

HARD NUMERIC THRESHOLDS — NO CLINICAL GRADATION

HR=100 → normal; HR=101 → critical. Real clinical practice uses continuous risk curves and patient baselines. A threshold-based system can miss deteriorating patients approaching (but not crossing) the boundary.

📊

CONFIDENCE SCORES ARE AUTHORIAL, NOT DATA-DRIVEN

Confidence values (e.g. 0.97 for Heart Attack) are hardcoded by the rule author, not derived from epidemiological data or Bayesian inference. They serve as tiebreakers but do not represent statistically validated probabilities.

🔗

NO TEMPORAL REASONING OR SYMPTOM DURATION

The system has no concept of onset, duration, or trends. A symptom present for 10 minutes is treated identically to one for 10 days, preventing recognition of evolving clinical presentations.

🧬

NO PATIENT DEMOGRAPHICS

Age, sex, weight, pregnancy status, medications, and allergies are absent. A 70-year-old with chest pain and a 25-year-old receive identical outputs, despite vastly different clinical risk profiles.
